      Natural-language Guided Cross-view Geo-localization (NGCG) aims to retrieve geo-tagged satellite imagery using textual descriptions of ground scenes. While recent NGCG methods commonly rely on CLIP-style dual-encoder architectures, they often suffer from weak cross-modal generalization. 
      <br /><br />
      In this work, we present a simple yet effective framework to <strong>adapt MLLMs for NGCG via parameter-efficient finetuning</strong>. Our approach optimizes latent representations within the MLLM while preserving its pretrained knowledge. Our method achieves SOTA on GeoText-1652 with a <strong>12.2% improvement</strong> in Recall@1, all while surpassing baselines with far fewer trainable parameters.
